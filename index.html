About

Physics, computation, and the patience to sit with difficult problems.

My work sits at the intersection of theoretical astrophysics, scientific computing, machine learning, and outreach.

I am studying for an MSc in Astrophysics at Queen Mary University of London, after completing a BSc in Physics with Astrophysics and Cosmology at King's College London.

I am especially interested in gravitation, cosmology, machine learning for physical systems, space weather, and the ways computation can make difficult physics more visible and testable.

Alongside research and coding, I care deeply about science communication and outreach. I have worked on space-science education, student workshops, public-facing talks, and projects designed to make technical ideas more accessible without flattening the physics.

Research

Post-Newtonian Effects and Tests of Gravity

My MSc dissertation explores how weak-field gravity is described, tested, constrained, and compared across general relativity and alternative theories.

Dissertation focus

I am working through the post-Newtonian and parametrized post-Newtonian formalisms, classical Solar-System tests of gravity, binary-pulsar applications, and the weak-field limit of Brans–Dicke theory. The project combines analytical derivations with Mathematica calculations and visualisations.
